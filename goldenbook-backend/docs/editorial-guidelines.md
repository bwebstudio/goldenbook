# Goldenbook — Editorial Guidelines

## Tone

- **Curatorial, not promotional.** We observe, we don't sell.
- **Precise, not vague.** Prefer a concrete detail over a generic adjective.
- **Brief.** One or two sentences. If a third is needed, earn it.
- **Understated.** The Monocle/Michelin register — confident, never loud.
- **Natural.** Each note should sound like it was written by a person who visited, not by a template.

## Goldenbook Notes

### Purpose
A Goldenbook Note is our editorial perspective on a place. It tells the reader *what to expect* in a way that description text doesn't.

### Rules
1. **Never repeat the description.** The note adds perspective, not summary.
2. **Never use "address" as a synonym for "place".** It translates to "dirección" in Spanish (literally: street address). Use: place, spot, restaurant, table, bar, kitchen, shop, etc.
3. **Vary sentence openings.** Avoid the pattern "A [adj] [type] in [city]" in more than 20% of notes.
4. **Avoid these words/phrases:**
   - "unhurried" — overused in editorial writing
   - "rewards" (rewards curiosity, rewards patience) — cliché
   - "considered" / "considered details" — vague
   - "well-curated" — every shop says this about itself
   - "ideal para…" / "perfecto para…" — promotional, not editorial
   - "un lugar agradable" — says nothing
   - "bien valorado por los visitantes" — TripAdvisor copy, not Goldenbook
5. **Each note should have personality.** If you can swap the note between two different restaurants and it still works, it's too generic.
6. **Reference concrete things:** the cooking style, the room, the lighting, the staff, the menu philosophy — not abstract qualities.

### Good examples
- "The menu doesn't change much. It doesn't need to."
- "Portuguese roots run deep here — the kitchen respects tradition without clinging to it."
- "Fewer pieces than you'd expect, each one chosen with a conviction that shows."
- "The collection here tells a story the city rarely tells about itself."

### Bad examples
- "A refined address in Lisbon, rooted in Portuguese tradition." ← generic, "address" problem
- "A well-curated shop with considered details." ← empty, cliché
- "A distinctive address that stands apart from the obvious choices." ← says literally nothing

## Insider Tips (Dicas Locais)

### Purpose
A tip that makes the reader feel like they know something others don't. It should be the kind of advice a friend who lives in the city would give.

### Rules
1. **Must be specific and actionable.** Not "arrive early" but "arrive before 10 — the pastries come out warm at 9:30."
2. **Never generic advice that applies to any place:**
   - ❌ "Reserve with anticipation"
   - ❌ "Arrive early"
   - ❌ "Ask for the wine of the house"
   - ❌ "Visit during off-peak hours"
3. **Reference concrete details:** table numbers, specific dishes, time windows, staff roles, hidden areas, specific wines/producers.
4. **One specific insight per tip.** Not a list of generic advice.
5. **Should make the reader feel like an insider**, not like they're reading a travel brochure.

### Good examples
- "The terrace only has six tables — call ahead, or the indoor room won't disappoint either."
- "The fish changes daily — ask what came in this morning before ordering from the menu."
- "Room 3 is where most visitors rush through — it's actually the strongest part."
- "The pieces in the back room rotate weekly — ask what just arrived."

### Bad examples
- "Ask the sommelier for a regional recommendation." ← applies to literally any restaurant with a sommelier
- "Visit in the first hour after opening." ← applies to any museum on earth
- "Worth spending a few extra minutes." ← says nothing

## Translation Rules

### General
- Translations are handled by DeepL (EN → PT, EN → ES).
- The **English source text must be written in a way that translates well.** Avoid idioms, wordplay, or words with ambiguous translations.

### Words to avoid in English source (because they translate badly):
| English | Bad ES translation | Use instead |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| "address" (as place) | "dirección" | place, spot, restaurant, table, shop |
| "honest" (as unpretentious) | "honesto" | straightforward, unpretentious, no-frills |
| "considered" (as thoughtful) | "considerado" | thoughtful, careful, deliberate |
| "a solid base" (hotel) | "una base sólida" | a dependable hotel, a good base |
| "rewards" (verb) | "recompensa" | is worth it, merits, earns |

### ES-specific issues
- "dirección" = street address, NOT a place to go. Use "lugar", "sitio", "parada", "referencia".
- "honesto" = morally honest, NOT unpretentious. Use "sin pretensiones", "sencillo".
- "considerado" = considerate (polite), NOT thoughtful. Use "cuidado", "pensado", "deliberado".

### PT-specific issues
- Maintain formal register (você, not tu) unless the context is very casual.
- "Dica" = tip/hint — correct. "Conselho" = advice — avoid for insider tips.
- Watch for Brazilian Portuguese leaking in from DeepL. Use European Portuguese markers.
